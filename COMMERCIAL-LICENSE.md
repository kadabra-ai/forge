## When you need a commercial license

The AGPL-3.0 requires that any derivative of Forge — whether you distribute it or offer it as a
network service — be released under the AGPL-3.0 with its complete source available to users.

You need a **commercial license** if you want to do any of the following **without** meeting that
obligation:

- Ship a **closed-source product** that includes or links Forge.
- Operate a **proprietary SaaS / network service** built on Forge without publishing its source.
- **Embed** Forge in a product you distribute without offering the corresponding source.
- Otherwise build on Forge under terms incompatible with the AGPL's copyleft.

You do **not** need a commercial license to use the unmodified tool, to use it internally, or to
build derivatives that you release as open source under the AGPL-3.0.

## What the commercial license grants

A commercial license is a negotiated agreement that lifts the AGPL's copyleft obligations for
your specified product or service, in exchange for a fee. Exact terms — scope, duration,
support, and pricing — are agreed per customer.
