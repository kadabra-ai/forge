# Licensing

Forge is **dual-licensed**. You may use it under either:

1. The **GNU Affero General Public License v3.0** (AGPL-3.0-only) — free of charge, see
   [`LICENSE`](LICENSE); or
2. A **commercial license** — see [`COMMERCIAL-LICENSE.md`](COMMERCIAL-LICENSE.md).

Pick the one that fits how you use Forge. This document explains, in plain language, which one
that is. It is a summary for convenience — the actual legal terms are the license files
themselves.

## TL;DR

| What you do | License | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Use the unmodified tool — even inside a company, for any purpose | AGPL-3.0 | Free |
| Modify Forge for your own internal use, not offered to others | AGPL-3.0 | Free |
| Build a derivative and **release it as open source** under AGPL-3.0 | AGPL-3.0 | Free |
| Build a derivative or a service and keep it **closed source** | Commercial | Paid |

## The AGPL path (free)

Under the AGPL you can do almost anything, with **one condition**: if you build on top of Forge
and make that available to others — by distributing it **or by running it as a network
service** — you must release the complete corresponding source of your work under the AGPL too.

This is "strong copyleft." It is what keeps Forge and everything built on it open.

You are free, at no cost, to:

- Run Forge for any purpose, including commercially, inside any organization.
- Study, modify, and self-host it.
- Distribute it, or a derivative, **provided your derivative is also AGPL-3.0 and its source is
  available to your users** — including users who reach it over a network (AGPL §13).

## The commercial path (paid)

If you want to build on Forge but **cannot or do not want to release your work under the
AGPL** — for example, a closed-source product, a proprietary SaaS, or an embedded component you
ship without source — you need a **commercial license**.

A commercial license lifts the AGPL's copyleft obligations for your product. See
[`COMMERCIAL-LICENSE.md`](COMMERCIAL-LICENSE.md) and contact
[licensing@kadabra.rs](mailto:licensing@kadabra.rs).

## Why a CLA is required to contribute

Dual-licensing only works if a single party holds the rights to relicense the whole codebase.
That is why every contributor signs a **Contributor License Agreement (CLA)** before their
contribution is merged. The CLA grants the project maintainer the rights needed to offer Forge
under both the AGPL and the commercial license.

Contributions are made voluntarily. See [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](CONTRIBUTING.md) for details.
